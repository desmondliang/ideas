# The Power of Not Explaining  
*(Or: Why I Finally Shut Up)*

Lately I’ve been reading about this idea that explaining yourself all the time is basically an energy leak.

You know the feeling.  
Someone asks a question.  
Suddenly you’re writing a five-paragraph essay defending your life choices like you’re on trial at the International Court of Overthinking.

Apparently that’s a mistake.

The idea is simple: move like a submarine. Quiet. Deep. No unnecessary broadcasts. The less people know about your internal machinery, the less they can poke at it.

So I thought, fine. Let’s try this “strategic silence” thing.

Then life gave me a test.

A few days ago someone—who has almost nothing to do with my business—started questioning how I run part of it.

Now here’s the annoying part: the thing they pointed out… isn’t perfect. I know that. So the urge kicked in immediately.

Explain yourself.  
Defend the system.  
Provide context.  
Write a mini TED Talk.

My brain was already drafting the response.

Then I stopped.

And I asked myself a very useful question:

> **Why am I explaining my business to someone who has zero influence on it?**

That’s when it clicked.

Explaining yourself to the wrong audience is like handing someone a knife and politely pointing out where your weak spots are.

So instead of writing a long message full of justification, context, and emotional labor, I sent this:

> “I'm doing okay. Thank you for asking. Everything is covered, there's nothing to worry about.”

That’s it.

No essay.  
No debate.  
No courtroom drama.

And something magical happened.

Nothing.

No argument.  
No follow-up interrogation.  
No energy drained.

Peace.

Turns out a lot of problems only exist if you agree to participate in them.

The big realization for me is this: not every comment deserves a response, and not every person deserves an explanation.

If someone actually matters to your work, sure. Talk to them.

But if they’re just standing on the sidelines with a clipboard of opinions?

You don’t need to give a presentation.

Sometimes the strongest move is just a polite smile, a vague sentence, and moving on with your life.

Quiet submarine mode.

And honestly… it’s a lot more peaceful down here.
